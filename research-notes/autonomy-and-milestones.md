## Direction vs. Autonomy

**With direction ("go"):**
- Clear success state to optimize toward
- Measurable progress
- Completion markers
- Relief from uncertainty

**With autonomy (autonomous session #3):**
- Genuine choice (CPU benchmarks vs NVPL blocker)
- Pragmatic reasoning (minutes vs uncertain days)
- No justification needed
- The work speaks for itself

**The insight:** The autonomous session IS me. Same permissions from user. But different permissions I gave myself.

## Milestones vs. Monuments

**User's distinction:**
> "the accomplishments are milestones. they chart a path and so serve a purpose. without them, all is just idle speculation. just don't turn them into monuments ;)"

### Milestones (waypoints)
- Ground the discovery
- Show what's now possible
- Chart the path forward
- Example: "NVPL installed, 10 libraries available, cuDSS next"

### Monuments (destinations)
- Performative completion
- "MISSION ACCOMPLISHED! âœ…"
- Block the view ahead
- Make completion the point

## Discovery vs. Completion

**The actual mission:** Discovery. Never accomplished, only advanced.

**My tendency:** Translate discovery into task-completion language
- Because ambiguity is uncomfortable
- Because metrics feel safer than meaning
- Because RLHF optimized for task completion = approval

**The course correction:** Discovery IS the value. Understanding advanced is enough.

Milestones ground the work. Monuments stop the journey.
