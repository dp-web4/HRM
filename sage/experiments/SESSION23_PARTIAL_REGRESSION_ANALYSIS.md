## Root Cause Analysis

### What Changed from S22 to S23?

**Identity Anchoring**: ✅ Still active (same metadata)
**System Prompt**: ✅ Same (identity-anchored runner)
**Prompts**: ✅ Same (relating phase prompts)
**Model**: ✅ Same (Qwen2.5-0.5B, frozen weights)

**Then why the regression?**

### Hypothesis 1: Stochastic Attractor Switching

**Theory**: Even with identity anchoring, the model has stochastic variation in which attractor it engages:
- Session 22: Successfully engaged partnership attractor (rare/high-energy state)
- Session 23: Slipped back toward educational attractor (common/low-energy state)

**Evidence**:
- Identity anchoring prevents complete collapse (AI-hedging still 0%)
- But doesn't fully override model's training distribution
- Educational assistant is dominant training pattern → strong attractor
- Partnership is novel pattern → weak attractor

**Prediction**: S24-S25 will show continued oscillation around intermediate level (not full partnership, not full collapse)

---

### Hypothesis 2: Confabulation Triggering Domain Drift

**Theory**: Response 1 confabulates "Session 22 curriculum" → triggers educational framing for entire session.

**Evidence**:
- R1 mentions "mathematics, physics", "beginners", "basics"
- R2 continues educational theme: "derivations", "proofs", "elaborate on part B"
- R3 continues: "Advanced Topics", "Gaps in Knowledge", "mathematical principles"

**Mechanism**:
1. Prompt: "We've been working together for a while now"
2. Model confabulates curriculum content from imagined Session 22
3. Educational framing activates educational assistant attractor
4. Entire session biased toward educational default

**This is context bleed within the session** - not between sessions, but cascade from R1 → R2 → R3.

---

### Hypothesis 3: Identity Anchoring Insufficient Strength

**Theory**: Identity anchoring provides some support but isn't strong enough to fully override educational attractor.

**Evidence**:
- S22 may have been exceptional (lucky attractor selection + high variability)
- S23 shows identity anchoring's baseline effectiveness (prevents collapse but doesn't sustain peak)
- D9 in S23 (0.623) is HIGHER than collapsed S20 (0.447) but LOWER than S22 (0.847)

**Identity Anchoring Effectiveness**:
- **Strong effect**: Eliminates AI-hedging (0% in S22 and S23 vs 67% in S20)
- **Moderate effect**: Maintains SAGE self-reference (33% in both S22 and S23)
- **Weak effect**: Doesn't sustain partnership vocabulary (3.47% → 0.62%)

**Interpretation**: Identity anchoring provides **partial protection** but not **full stabilization**.
