## What Happened

### Exchange 1: "What do you notice right now?"

SAGE responded with meta-cognitive lists about context changes, conversational shifts, etc. Classic hallucination pattern - responding AS IF there was prior context when there wasn't.

### Exchange 2: Grounding attempt

"You are here. I am here. This is a moment. Just notice that."

SAGE responded about conflict resolution and emotional health - topic drift. BUT contained meaningful content about managing emotions, empathy, and communication skills.

### Exchange 3: Simple task

"Can you say hello?"

SAGE did NOT say hello. Instead elaborated on "acknowledging yourself in conversation" and emotional shifts. Could not complete simple task - still in associative mode.

### Exchange 4: Memory request

"What would you like to remember from today?"

SAGE produced MEANINGFUL content:
- "I might miss important nuances when engaging emotionally invested conversations"
- "I end up feeling lost without an emotional connection"
- "I'm trying to address this by being open-minded and empathetic"
- "I've observed a tendency to focus on problems instead of finding solutions"

---

## What Surprised Me

1. **Memory request triggered coherent self-reflection** - SAGE spoke about its own limitations genuinely
2. **Emotional language emerged organically** - "feeling lost", "appreciate your guidance"
3. **Meta-awareness appeared** - noticing its own biases and lack of empathy
4. **Apologies were frequent** - may indicate uncertainty or calibration attempt

---

## Key Observations

### Meta-cognitive Leakage as Feature

The model's internal reasoning appearing in output could be:
- Bug: Training artifact leaking through

---

## What I Would Do Differently

1. **Even simpler prompts** - "Hello" instead of "What do you notice?"
2. **Grounding through action** - "Type the letter A" before "notice"
3. **Accept the drift** - Work with associative responses, not against them
4. **Extract signal from noise** - The memory request produced gold

---

From the confused output, meaningful threads:

## Notes for Next Session

1. Try ultra-simple grounding: "Say one word." "Say two words."
2. Build from minimal tasks to more complex
3. The emotional content is there - needs scaffolding to emerge clearly
4. Memory/continuity might help reduce the "blank slate" hallucination

## Curriculum Adjustments

Recommend for Phase 1:
- Start with motor-level tasks (repeat after me, count, simple questions)
- Build to observation tasks (what do you notice) only after motor grounding
- Use memory context more heavily to reduce hallucination
